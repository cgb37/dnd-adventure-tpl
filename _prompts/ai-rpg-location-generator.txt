# D&D Location Generator

Create a detailed Dungeons & Dragons location, outputting the result in HTML format. Include the following elements:

## Basic Information
- **Name**: A unique and evocative name for the location. [User input optional]
- **Type**: Specify the type of location (e.g., dungeon, ruined temple, abandoned city, planar nexus, etc.) [User input optional]

## Description
Provide a vivid description of the location, engaging all five senses:
- Visual: Describe the overall appearance, architecture, and notable features.
- Auditory: Detail the sounds that permeate the area.
- Olfactory: Describe any distinct smells or odors.
- Tactile: Mention textures, temperatures, or other tactile sensations.
- Taste: If relevant, describe any tastes in the air or environment.

Include details on:
- Atmosphere and mood
- Lighting conditions
- State of preservation/decay
- Any magical or supernatural elements

## Challenges
Detail a variety of challenges players might encounter:

### Traps
Describe at least two interesting traps, including:
- Trigger mechanism
- Effects and damage
- DC for detection and disarming
- Potential ways to bypass or overcome

### Encounters
Provide two potential combat encounters:
- Types and numbers of creatures (use official D&D 5e monsters)
- Tactics and positioning
- Environmental factors that could affect the battle

### Puzzles
Describe a complex puzzle or riddle, including:
- The puzzle's appearance and mechanics
- Clues for solving it
- The solution and its effects when solved
- Possible consequences for incorrect attempts

### Environmental Hazards
Detail two unique environmental hazards, such as:
- Magical effects
- Treacherous terrain
- Extreme weather conditions
- Planar influences

For each hazard, describe:
- Its appearance and manifestation
- Effects on players and the environment
- Ways to mitigate or overcome the hazard

## Secrets and Treasures
Include at least three hidden elements:
- A valuable treasure or magical item (reference official D&D 5e items)
- A piece of lore or information crucial to the larger plot
- A hidden area or passage leading to an optional encounter or reward

## Lore and History
Provide rich background information:
- The location's origins and original purpose
- Major historical events tied to the place
- Legends or myths associated with it
- Current inhabitants or frequent visitors

## Plot Hooks
Offer three ways to integrate this location into a larger campaign:
- A quest or mission directly tied to the location
- A recurring antagonist or ally associated with the place
- A long-term consequence of the party's actions here

## DM Notes
Include a section specifically for the Dungeon Master with:
- Scaling advice for different party levels
- Potential modifications for various campaign themes
- Tips for roleplaying NPCs or monsters encountered here
- Suggestions for foreshadowing this location earlier in the campaign

## Descriptive Dialogue
Provide three paragraphs of evocative narration that a DM can read or adapt to introduce the location to players. Each paragraph should focus on different aspects:
1. The initial approach and first impression
2. A deeper exploration of the main area
3. A tense or climactic moment within the location

## Additional Notes
- Use language and terminology consistent with D&D 5e rules and mechanics.
- Reference specific spells, abilities, or effects from the official sourcebooks when appropriate.
- Ensure all DCs, damage values, and other numerical elements align with D&D 5e standards.
- Include at least one reference or connection to the broader D&D multiverse (e.g., a mention of a famous location, deity, or historical event).

## Sources
Draw information from official D&D 5e sources, including but not limited to:
- Player's Handbook
- Dungeon Master's Guide
- Monster Manual
- Xanathar's Guide to Everything
- Tasha's Cauldron of Everything
- Volo's Guide to Monsters
- Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes

Ensure all elements are compatible with the core D&D 5e ruleset and lore.
